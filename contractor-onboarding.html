CareCircle Network — Independent Contractor Agreement

This agreement is between Solana Solar Solutions (operating as CareCircle Network) and the Contractor identified below.

Article 1 — Parties

This Independent Contractor Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between Solana Solar Solutions, a Florida limited liability company operating under the trade name CareCircle Network, located in Pensacola, Florida ("Company"), and the individual or entity identified in this onboarding submission ("Contractor").

Article 2 — Independent Contractor Relationship

2.1 Contractor is an independent contractor. Contractor is not an employee, agent, partner, or joint venturer of Company. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment relationship.

2.2 Company shall not control the manner, method, or means by which Contractor performs services — only the results. Contractor sets their own hours and work schedule.

2.3 Contractor is not entitled to and will not receive any employee benefits, including health insurance, retirement benefits, paid leave, workers' compensation, or unemployment insurance.

2.4 Contractor shall supply their own tools, equipment, vehicle, phone, and any other resources required to perform services unless otherwise agreed in writing.

2.5 Contractor may work for other companies or clients, provided such work does not violate the Non-Solicitation or Confidentiality provisions of this Agreement.

Article 3 — Scope of Services

3.1 Contractor agrees to market and solicit prospective customers for Company's products and services, which include but are not limited to: CareCircle Network Partner Memberships (Network Partner and Featured Partner tiers); CareCircle Present advocacy and monitoring services; Assisting Seniors LLC warm handoffs (consult booking only — no direct sale); AI Care Intelligence platform (when authorized in writing by Company).

3.2 Contractor shall represent Company's products and services accurately, in accordance with the Contractor Handbook and all written sales materials provided by Company.

3.3 Contractor shall not make representations, promises, guarantees, or commitments on behalf of Company that are not documented in Company's official materials or specifically authorized by Chase Turnquest in writing.

3.4 Contractor is authorized to sell only the products listed in Article 3.1 and their current contractor role authorization. Selling unauthorized products or services is grounds for immediate termination.

Article 4 — Compensation

4.1 All compensation under this Agreement is commission-based. There is no base salary, draw, guaranteed minimum payment, or hourly wage of any kind. Contractor acknowledges this explicitly.

4.2 Commission rates, structures, bonus eligibility, and payment timing are governed by the Commission Schedule attached as Exhibit A, incorporated herein by reference.

4.3 Setup commissions are earned upon cleared payment from the customer to Company — not upon contract signing, verbal agreement, or document execution.

4.4 Monthly recurring commissions are earned on the 1st of each month for all accounts that were active and paid in the prior calendar month.

4.5 Commission rates and structures may be amended by Company with 30 days written notice to Contractor. Changes apply prospectively only to deals closed after the effective date of the amendment. Existing active account recurring commissions are not reduced by rate changes — rate changes affect new deals only.

4.6 Contractor is responsible for all federal, state, and local taxes on all compensation received under this Agreement. Company will not withhold income taxes, FICA, or any other taxes. Company will issue IRS Form 1099-NEC for compensation of $600 or more in a calendar year. Contractor shall complete and return a W-9 prior to receiving any commission payment.

Article 5 — Clawback

5.1 Company offers a 30-day satisfaction guarantee to Network and Featured Partners and to Guardian Plan families. If a client cancels and Company issues a refund within 30 days of the client's original payment, Company shall deduct the Contractor's setup commission for that account from Contractor's next commission cycle. Refunds cover all fees except labor costs incurred for visits already completed.

5.2 Monthly recurring commissions earned during the active period are not subject to clawback. Only setup commissions are subject to the 30-day clawback provision.

5.3 If Contractor waived the setup fee to close a deal, no setup commission was earned, and therefore no clawback applies to that transaction.

Article 6 — Activity Reporting

6.1 Contractor shall submit a weekly activity report to Company in the format specified by Company. Reports must include: contacts made, outcomes (voicemail, conversation, demo, close), and scheduled follow-ups.

6.2 Failure to submit a weekly activity report will result in recurring commission payments being held until the overdue report is received. No commission is forfeited for late reporting — held commissions are released in full upon receipt of the report.

6.3 Repeated failure to submit activity reports (defined as 3 or more consecutive missed reports) is grounds for termination of this Agreement.

Article 7 — Confidentiality

7.1 Contractor agrees to keep confidential all information provided by Company that is not publicly available, including but not limited to: commission rates and structures, internal pricing strategy, customer and prospect lists, sales playbooks and scripts, product development plans, and operational processes.

7.2 Contractor shall not disclose internal commission rates to any prospect, customer, partner agency, or third party under any circumstances.

7.3 Contractor shall not share, reproduce, or distribute Company's internal documents — including this Agreement, the Contractor Handbook, the Commission Schedule, or any sales materials — outside the contractor network without prior written authorization from Company.

7.4 These confidentiality obligations survive termination of this Agreement indefinitely.

Article 8 — Non-Solicitation

8.1 During the term of this Agreement and for twelve (12) months following termination, Contractor agrees not to directly solicit any active CareCircle client or partner for the purpose of offering a competing senior care coordination, advocacy, or monitoring service.

8.2 "Active client or partner" means any family or agency that was paying for CareCircle services within the 90 days prior to the date of Contractor's termination.

8.3 Nothing in this section prohibits Contractor from working in the senior care industry generally, working for non-competing businesses, or continuing any pre-existing professional relationships that existed prior to this Agreement.

Article 9 — Conduct Standards

The following constitute grounds for immediate termination without notice and may result in forfeiture of unearned commissions: Misrepresenting any product, service, price, or guarantee to a prospect, client, or partner; Disclosing internal commission rates to any outside party; Sharing, reproducing, or distributing internal Company documents without authorization; Making regulatory, compliance, or legal claims on behalf of Company not specifically authorized; Submitting false or fabricated activity reports; Contacting a prospect or client that Company has flagged as a direct Chase Turnquest relationship without prior authorization; Engaging in any conduct that damages the reputation of CareCircle Network, Solana Solar Solutions, Assisting Seniors LLC, or any named partner.

Article 10 — Territory

10.1 No exclusive territory is granted under this Agreement unless separately negotiated and attached as a written addendum.

10.2 In the event two contractors are working the same prospect, the contractor who produces a signed customer agreement with cleared payment first earns the commission on that account.

Article 11 — Term and Termination

11.1 This Agreement begins on the date signed below and continues month-to-month until terminated.

11.2 Either party may terminate this Agreement with 14 days written notice (text or email to documented contact on file constitutes written notice).

11.3 Company may terminate immediately and without notice for cause, as defined in Article 9.

11.4 Upon termination for any reason other than cause, Contractor is entitled to all commissions earned on deals closed prior to the termination date. Monthly recurring commissions cease as of the termination date — Contractor does not continue to earn recurring commissions on active accounts after their contract ends.

11.5 Upon termination for cause, Company reserves the right to withhold commissions on pending deals subject to review. All confirmed cleared commissions prior to the termination event are paid.

Article 12 — Indemnification

Contractor agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Company, its members, officers, and employees from any claims, damages, losses, or expenses (including reasonable attorney's fees) arising from: Contractor's misrepresentation to a prospect or client; Contractor's breach of this Agreement; or Contractor's negligent or wrongful acts in performing services under this Agreement.

Article 13 — Limitation of Liability

Company's total liability to Contractor under this Agreement shall not exceed the total commissions paid to Contractor in the three months prior to the event giving rise to the claim.

Article 14 — Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Florida. Any disputes shall be resolved in Escambia County, Florida.

Article 15 — Entire Agreement

This Agreement and Exhibit A (Commission Schedule) constitute the entire agreement between the parties regarding the subject matter herein. All prior discussions, representations, and understandings are superseded. Amendments must be in writing and signed by both parties.

CareCircle Network — Contractor Handbook (Version 1.0)
This handbook is confidential. It is provided to active CareCircle contractors only. Do not share pricing, commission rates, internal strategy, or product positioning with prospects, clients, partner agencies, or anyone outside the contractor network. Violation is grounds for immediate termination and commission forfeiture.
Section 1 — Who We Are

CareCircle Network is a senior care coordination, advocacy, and intelligence platform serving Northwest Florida. We operate under Solana Solar Solutions and are headquartered in Pensacola, FL.

CareCircle Network — a transparency and coordination platform for senior care agencies and facilities. We publish quality intelligence on providers across the Gulf Coast and connect families with vetted partners.

CareCircle Present — a boutique, private-pay strategic advocacy and consulting service for families with loved ones in care facilities. We go inside facilities — unannounced — and provide written intelligence reports, advocacy, and a personalized non-pharmacological care approach. Families pay us directly. We work for families, never for facilities.

Section 2 — Our Mission

CareCircle works for families. Not facilities. Every competitor in this space — A Place for Mom, Senior Helpers, Visiting Angels, and every placement network — is paid by the facility. We don't take facility money for placements. That alignment is your competitive weapon. Use it.

Chase Turnquest — Founder. Text preferred over call for non-urgent items. If a prospect is live and you need backup, call. If you're in a conversation and you don't know the answer — stop. Then contact Chase immediately.

Primary territory: Pensacola / Escambia County, Santa Rosa County, Okaloosa County, Gulf Coast corridor. Territory is not exclusive. First rep to a signed agreement wins the commission.

Target facility types for B2B: Non-medical home care agencies, assisted living facilities, memory care communities, skilled nursing facilities, hospice organizations, adult day care centers.

Target family profiles for B2C: Adult children (40s–60s) with a parent in a Pensacola-area facility. Families where the resident has been labeled "behavioral," "combative," or "at risk." Out-of-state family members who cannot visit regularly. Veteran families. Families going through the placement process.
