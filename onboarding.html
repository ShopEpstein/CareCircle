Section 01
Contractor Roles & Revenue Lines

CareCircle operates three revenue streams. Contractors can sell into one or all three. Each has its own comp structure. Highest earners work all three.

🤝
Network Partner Sales
$52–$122/mo recurring per deal
Sell monthly agency/facility memberships to home care agencies, ALFs, memory care facilities, and hospice. B2B direct sales. These deals compound — every partner you sign keeps paying you every month they're active. Primary revenue stream for most reps.
🛡️
CareCircle Present Sales
$52–$134/mo recurring per family
Sell family-paid monitoring, advocacy, and strategic consulting services directly to families with loved ones in care facilities. Single visits or monthly Guardian Plans. Human to human. High emotional stakes. Higher close rate for empathetic reps.
🤖
AI Care Intelligence (SaaS)
$35–$70/mo recurring per facility
Sell the memoia.live AI Care Intelligence platform to Featured Partners and facilities. Per-resident SaaS tool. Sold as add-on to Featured Partner deals or standalone to facilities not yet in the network. Requires product-specific training.
🎯
Full Stack Rep (All Three)
Uncapped — compound recurring book
Best earners sell all three products. A single facility relationship can generate a Network membership, AI Care Intelligence SaaS, AND family referrals for CareCircle Present. One relationship, three revenue lines. This is the play.

Section 06
Commission Rules & Clawback Policy

Payment Schedule

  • ✓
    Setup commissions — paid when payment clears in CareCircle's account. Not on signing, not on contract execution. On cleared payment. Typically within 3–5 business days of collection.
  • ✓
    Monthly recurring commissions — paid on the 1st of each month for all partners who were active and paid in the prior month.
  • ✓
    Bonuses — calculated at month/quarter end and paid with the next regular commission cycle.
  • ✓
    Payment method — ACH direct deposit (preferred) or Zelle for amounts under $500. All commission statements sent to contractor email on file.

Clawback Policy

⚠️
60-Day Clawback Window
CareCircle offers a 60-day money-back guarantee to all partners. If a partner cancels and requests a refund within 60 days of signup, and CareCircle issues the refund, the contractor's setup commission on that deal is clawed back from the next commission cycle. Monthly recurring commissions earned during the active period are not clawed back — only the setup commission.
ScenarioSetup CommissionRecurring Commission
Partner stays active >60 days Kept — fully earned Paid monthly as long as active
Partner cancels within 60 days + refund issued Clawback — deducted next cycle Any earned monthly commissions kept
Partner cancels after 60 days (no refund) Kept Stops — no more recurring
Setup fee was waived at rep's discretion No commission (nothing to clawback) Recurring still earns normally

Important Rules

  • Setup fee negotiation: You may reduce or waive the setup fee to close a deal — this is your primary negotiating lever. If you waive it, you waive your commission on it. Never advertise this option publicly.
  • No double-dipping: If Chase or another CareCircle team member closes a deal you introduced but did not close, introductions may qualify for a reduced referral credit (10%) but not full commission. Close your own deals.
  • Territory: No exclusive territories. First-to-close wins. If two reps are working the same agency, the rep who creates a signed agreement first earns the commission.
  • Activity minimum for recurring payout: Contractors must submit a monthly activity report (calls, visits, outcomes) to remain eligible for recurring commission payout. Zero activity reported = commissions held pending report submission.
  • 1099 threshold: CareCircle will issue a 1099-NEC for all contractors earning $600+ in a calendar year. Contractors are responsible for all self-employment taxes. CareCircle does not withhold.
Section 07
Contractor Onboarding Checklist

Complete before making your first outreach call. Steps are in order — don't skip. The playbook only works if you've walked the platform from a prospect's perspective first.

Before Day 1 — Admin

  • Pre-Start
    Sign 1099 Independent Contractor Agreement — nothing starts before this is executed and returned. No exceptions.
  • Pre-Start
    Submit W-9 — name exactly as it appears on your tax return, SSN or EIN, address. Required for commission payments.
  • Pre-Start
    Submit banking info for ACH — routing and account number, or confirm Zelle phone number for early payments under $500.
  • Pre-Start
    Confirm commission tier — you start at Silver. Acknowledge you've read this document and understand the tier upgrade thresholds.

Day 1 — Platform & Product

  • Day 1
    Receive and read the full Sales Operations Manual — sections 1–4 minimum before your Day 2 call. This is your bible.
  • Day 1
    Walk carecircle.fit/research — search 3 different providers on your own. See exactly what a prospective agency sees when they search their own name. This is your door-opener.
  • Day 1
    Set up call log — Google Sheet or CRM provided. Columns: Agency Name, Contact Name, Phone, Date Called, Outcome, Follow-Up Date. You log every single call.
  • Day 1
    Walk the CareCircle Present page — read every tier, every guarantee, every FAQ. You need to be able to answer any family question cold.

Day 2 — Training & Role-Play

  • Day 2
    Playbook quiz call with Chase — 15 minutes. You will be asked: What's the 60-day guarantee? What does A Place for Mom charge? What are the two partner tiers and pricing? Don't wing it.
  • Day 2
    Role-play the B2B pitch — twice — Chase plays a skeptical ALF marketing director. You run the full call. Debrief after each. You don't get on real calls until you've done this.
  • Day 2
    Pull your first hit list — identify 20 high-priority agencies to call first. Write them down with phone numbers. Your first week target is 50 calls minimum.

Days 3–5 — Live Calls

  • Day 3
    Shadow Chase on 2 live calls — listen only. No talking. Debrief immediately after each call.
  • Day 3
    Make first 5 solo calls with Chase available — Chase doesn't speak, but is on the line or immediately reachable. Debrief after each.
  • Day 5
    First weekly activity report — calls made, outcomes (DM, VM, callback, demo scheduled, closed), follow-ups booked. This report is required for commission eligibility.

30-Day Review

  • Day 30
    Performance review call — calls made, demos given, deals closed. Are you on pace? What's working? Adjust territory or approach as needed.
  • Day 30
    Commission statement review — we walk through every line item together. Any disputes resolved here, not in email.
  • Day 30
    Unlock CareCircle Present selling — if you've closed at least 2 Network Partner deals by day 30, you're cleared to add CareCircle Present to your pitch. Not before.

INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR AGREEMENT
This Independent Contractor Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of the date signed below, between TransBid LLC, a Florida limited liability company, operating under the name CareCircle Network ("Company"), and the undersigned independent contractor ("Contractor").

1. Relationship of the Parties

Contractor is an independent contractor and not an employee, agent, partner, or joint venturer of Company. Contractor shall have no authority to bind Company in any contract, agreement, or obligation. Company shall not control the manner or means by which Contractor performs services, only the result.

2. Services

Contractor agrees to solicit prospective customers for Company's services, including but not limited to: CareCircle Network Partner Memberships, CareCircle Present advocacy services, and AI Care Intelligence SaaS products (collectively, "Products"). Contractor shall present Company's Products in accordance with provided sales materials and shall not make representations inconsistent with Company's published pricing, service descriptions, or guarantees.

3. Compensation — Commission Only

Contractor acknowledges and agrees that all compensation is commission-based only. There is no base salary, draw, or guaranteed minimum payment of any kind.

Commissions are calculated per the Commission Schedule attached as Exhibit A and incorporated herein by reference. Commission rates, tier thresholds, bonus structures, and payout timing are subject to change by Company with 30 days written notice. Changes apply prospectively only to deals closed after the effective date of the change.

Setup commissions are earned upon cleared payment from the customer. Monthly recurring commissions are earned on the 1st of each month for partners active and paid in the prior month. No commission is earned on refunded transactions.

4. Clawback

If Company issues a refund to a customer pursuant to its published guarantee policy within sixty (60) days of that customer's original payment, Company may deduct the Contractor's setup commission for that transaction from Contractor's next commission cycle. Monthly recurring commissions earned during the active period are not subject to clawback.

5. Confidentiality

Contractor agrees to maintain the confidentiality of Company's pricing structures, internal commission rates, customer lists, sales materials, and operational processes. Contractor shall not disclose commission rates to prospects, customers, or third parties. This obligation survives termination of this Agreement.

6. Non-Solicitation

For twelve (12) months following termination of this Agreement, Contractor agrees not to solicit Company's active customers on behalf of a competing senior care referral, advocacy, or care coordination platform.

7. Taxes

Contractor is solely responsible for all federal, state, and local taxes on compensation received under this Agreement, including self-employment taxes. Company will not withhold income taxes, Social Security, or Medicare. Company will issue Form 1099-NEC for total compensation of $600 or more in a calendar year.

8. Termination

Either party may terminate this Agreement with 14 days written notice. Company may terminate immediately for cause, including but not limited to: misrepresentation to customers, breach of confidentiality, or failure to submit required activity reports. Upon termination, Contractor is entitled to all earned commissions for deals closed prior to the termination date. Recurring commissions cease upon termination.

9. Entire Agreement

This Agreement, together with Exhibit A (Commission Schedule), constitutes the entire agreement between the parties regarding the subject matter herein and supersedes all prior discussions, representations, and agreements.
